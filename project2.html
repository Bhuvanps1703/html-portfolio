Kumara Parvatha

Kumara Parvatha, also known as Pushpagiri is the second-highest peak in Coorg, Karnataka. Renowned for its challenging trek, the peak stands at 1,712 meters above sea level. The trail offers diverse landscapes, including dense forests and meadows, and the trek covers approximately14 to 16 kilometers each way from the popular starting point at Kukke Subramanya Temple. The region is known for its scenic beauty, biodiversity, and religious significance, making it a sought-after destination for nature enthusiasts and trekkers.

Things to Before Going

  • Permits: Verify if permits are needed
  • Fitness: Assess and improve physical fitness.
  • Pack Light: Carry essentials only.
  • Hydration: Bring sufficient water and snacks.
  • Clothing: Wear appropriate and comfortable attire.
  • Footwear: Use sturdy trekking shoes.
  • Navigation: Familiarize with the trail and carry a map or GPS.
  • Gear: Bring necessary trekking equipment and a first aid kit.
  • Group Trekking: Preferably trek in groups.
  • Leave No Trace: Follow environmental preservation principles.
  • Accommodation: Plan for accommodation if staying overnight.
  • Early Start: Begin the trek early for daylight safety.
  • Wildlife: Be aware and respectful of local wildlife.
  • Emergency Contacts: Have a list of emergency contacts.
  • Check Weather: Avoid extreme conditions.
